What Are The Most Helpful Road Trip Advice 

By The Author
June 3, 2023

Road trips are a completely different kind of travel experience. You can get entirely in tune with the destination, dive deep, discover hidden gems along the way, and immerse in the area instead of just arriving. These top tips for road trips will prepare you long before you lock your front door and help you save money so you can spend more time enjoying the trip and less dealing with pesky complications. 

Be Smart About Gas Mileage

As the prices of gas continue to increase, you may find yourself spending more on gas and having less spending money on the road. To ensure you are saving on gas, one thing you can do is decelerate. For instance, when going from 0-50 mph, slow down your vehicle’s acceleration time to about 15 seconds, and you can save up to 30% (or more!) on gas mileage. You may also keep your windows shut while driving at high speeds to avoid decreasing your gas mileage by up to 10%. 

Removing excess weight, avoiding driving on rough terrain, and ensuring tires are pressurized to their maximum limit are also actions you can make to save money on gas. 

Make Sure Your Vehicle Is Ready

 

Be prepared for anything. The road can be very dangerous for road travelers so you have to create a plan at least a few weeks or months in advance for any possible difficulties and repairs. Check and adjust your car oil, tire pressure, spare tire, coolant, brakes, accessories, and lights if needed. If you are not very knowledgeable about car parts, get your vehicle inspected my auto repair professionals. 

Make Sure You Are Ready

 

You certainly have to be just as ready for the road trip as your vehicle. Tell your close friends and family where you are heading, and make sure to get a good night’s sleep before hitting the road. Check on your cellphone range and make sure you have the number of emergency operators and you can reach them from where you are planning to go. 

It is also a must for road travelers to become familiar with the roadways and the weather forecast. Utilize the top traveling apps to ensure you are properly ready for the road trip. Also, remember to wear a comfortable outfit and pack necessities, including first aid kit, snacks, drinking water, jumper cables, and maps, among many others. 

Avoid peak dinner time and rush hour traffic

 

Sitting in a congested road and waiting for your order can definitely lose a lot of your time. We recommend eating breakfast after 9 am, getting lunch around 2 pm, and ordering dinner either early at 4 pm or waiting until 7 pm. During these timeframes, you will generally get seated faster, and your food will be served relatively quicker. 

When driving between destinations, on the other hand, avoid hitting the road from 7 am to 9 am and 4 pm to 6 pm. Use a mobile navigation app to spot potential traffic jams from construction, accidents, and congestions and find routes for detours.
